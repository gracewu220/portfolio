In my final project for the Advanced GIS course, I analyzed the connections among soil types, waste facilities, and wetlands in Florida to determine which wetlands are at the highest risk from water runoff and need conservation attention. To accomplish this, I utilized a range of ArcGIS tools, including Distance Accumulation, Reclassify, Fuzzy Membership, and Fuzzy Overlay. While the final map showcased the vulnerability of wetlands to waste and water runoff, it appeared to be another visualization of waste facilities rather than the vulnerability of wetlands. To improve the project, I would use the soil type data as an Input Barrier layer in Distance Accumulation, instead of using it as a separate variable in the overall vulnerability map.
